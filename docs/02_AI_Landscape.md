2. The AI Model Landscape (2024–2026)
The current AI landscape is not a single linear path, but rather a collection of multiple evolutionary branches
. Most frontier systems are adopting hybrid architectures that mix an MoE core, RAG memory, an agent loop, tool use, multimodal input, and multi-agent coordination
.
Core Architectures
Dense Transformer Models: These models (e.g., early GPT, Llama 1) use one unified neural network to solve everything
.
Mixture of Experts (MoE): Models like Mixtral and DeepSeek prioritize efficiency through specialization and routing
.
Agentic Systems: AI copilots that operate on a "plan → act → observe → iterate" loop to achieve autonomy
.
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG): Connects a model to an external knowledge store, reducing hallucinations and allowing smaller models to outperform larger ones on specific domains
.
Tool-Using Models: The model acts as a controller that decides when to use external APIs, search, or calculators to achieve precision in areas where pure language models fail
.
Small Language Models (SLMs): Models like Phi and Gemma aim for high intelligence per parameter to optimize learning for edge device deployment
.
Multimodal Models: Systems built with shared latent representations and cross-attention layers to process text, image, audio, and video, bringing AI closer to real sensory intelligence
.
World Models: An emerging direction focused on simulation
. Instead of predicting text, these models predict how the world behaves, which is crucial for robotics and planning
.
Multi-Agent Systems: Multiple AI agents collaborate or debate (e.g., a researcher agent, planner agent, and reviewer agent), proving that intelligence emerges socially
.
Reasoning-Optimized Models: Focuses on depth, step-by-step thinking, and verification
.
